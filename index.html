Why Life Expectancy Matters?

    Life Expectancy is a statistical measure of the estimate of the span of a life. The video can give you a quick understanding of how we select the criteria which is at birth. Life Expectancy is the critical element when it comes to life insurance. The insurance company will gather some sensitive information to predict the life expectancy of the policyholder and then calculate the premium that the policyholder needs to pay annually. Behind that the insurance company can ensure they have sufficient money when any accident happens (i.e. the policyholder dies before the estimation.) This would also be important to the policymakers, as you will find out that the average living age is growing, so we have a later retirement age.
    Here, we focus on the past data which is not estimated but actual data to understand the current trend of Life Expectancy and have a proper guess for the future trend as well.

Life Expectancy vs Adult Mortality from 2010 to 2015

When we talk about life span, we may also think of the opposite which is Mortality. There are various types of Mortality, such as Infant Death, Mortality Rate, Adult Mortality, etc. In this section, we focus on the negative relationship between Life Expectancy and Adult Mortality, which can give you a basic sense of how these two variables are related to each other. Before we look into it, I'd like to introduce the definition of Adult Mortality. It's the probability of dying between 15 and 60 years per 1000 population.
